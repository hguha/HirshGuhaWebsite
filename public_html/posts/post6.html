The Pita Pizza
recipe
25th January, 2021

As with the majority, I love pizza. Unfortunately pizza is not a dish that lends itself well to the home kitchen. It's hours of prep, what with proofing a yeast-leavened dough that can be strenuous to knead without proper equipment. In addition, a properly blistered crust has to be cooked at temperatures far exceeding the average home oven, either requiring an hour of preheating a pizza stone/steel at the highest temperature, or buying an expensive pizza oven.

What if I told you that you can make a delivious, albiet very non-traditional, pizza in about 15 minutes, start to finish, and for only $1?

The trick? A pita, naan, tortilla, or another flat bread. It solves the issue of needing high temperatures, requiring a ton of work to create them, and you can buy 8 of them at the store for like, $2.

Prep: 20mins, Cook: 3hrs

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup toppings to cook(think pepperoni, sausage, mushrooms, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup fresh toppingsthink cream cheese, basil, garlic, tomato slices, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup of shredded or fresh mozzerella cheese
  • 1/2 cup of marinara sauce
  • 1 flatbread(I use tortilla)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees. Place flatbread on a appropriate sized baking sheet.
  2. Place flatbread on a appropriate sized baking sheet.
  3. Spread marinara sauce edge to edge on the pizza, leaving as much uncovered bread as you like for the crust.
  4. Add the cheese and toppings that need to be cooked, and once the oven is preheated, place it in for 10 minutes.
  5. Once the pizza is out, immediately add fresh toppings, let rest for 5 minutes, and cut into 4 slices to serve.

The Formula

What I sought to provide here is not an exact recipe to necessarily follow to the tee, but rather a schematic for an easy weeknight meal. The formula is:
  1. flat bread - naan, tortilla, pita, etc.
  2. Sauce - marinara, alfredo, hummus, olive oil, etc.
  3. Cooked Toppings - mushrooms, pepperoni, sausage, chicken
  4. Fresh Toppings - Basil, parsley, spinach, tomato, onion, etc.
No matter what wild choices you make with each of the 4 categories, there's an extremely high probability that the dish will come out tasty. It's unbelievably cheap, very low calorie compared to a traditional pizza, and so fast to make. Give it a shot!
